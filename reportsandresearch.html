Research on Gender equality Is So Famous, But Why?

You would wonder why exactly should we invest time and resources in this kind of research.
Now, that's a very-much valid question. Are you not sure about the answer? Don't worry, we've got you covered. We will get to that after we talk about the term discrimination; so keep reading!

Discrimination is a word we seem to encounter a lot. If you're not familiar with the term, a quick google search will tell you that “unfairness” is a similar, more simple term. To be honest, all of us yearn for an equal state, opportunities, and rights regardless of our age, race, gender identity, disability, or sexual orientation. Discrimination is what we term it when we don't meet those expectations.

This point forth, we will talk about what we wish everyone knew about Gender Based Descrimination.

"Gender Inequality" is an issue that affects people across the globe. One would assume that as a "progressed" society, we would no longer have to cope with so much discrimination, but clearly, that's not the case. Unfortunately, even in the 21st century, men and women are yet to experience equality in most cases.

" If we invest in girls and women, the world and all of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals will advance forward rapidly as a result. "

— Ashley Judd, actor and UNFPA Goodwill Ambassador

We firmly believe that a society can progress and achieve higher development growth ONLY when both men and women enjoy equality.
However, take a look!

  • 64% of illiterate adults are women.That’s 2 out of 3.

  • Every day 39,000 girls are being into early marriage.That’s 26 girls for one minute.

  • 95% of the countries have a male lead head of state.

  • More than 100 countries have laws on the books that restrict women’s participation in the economy.
Not so pleasant, is it?

Now, this is where we go back to our initial question. Research on Gender equality Is So Famous, But Why?

“Data will tell us what to do, and if what we are doing is right. Data essentially tells us whether we are achieving the objectives and what needs to change in what we do”

— Papa Seck, Policy Specialist under Research and Data, UN Women


Subscribe to UN Women Official YouTube channel here.

Over the years, so much research has been done on this topic. Results and those research data are being used for various projects, campaigns, etc. We are happy to say that the change is happening. Slowly, but it's there.

Educate yourself and your community on these matters. Stand against any sort of discrimination. No matter how small your action is, it makes a difference.
